How to Use OptimizeAI

Detailed guide to getting the most out of OptimizeAI

Teacher Profile Selection

Using Preset Profiles

We offer several preset teacher profiles based on common marking styles. Select the one that best matches your teacher:

  • Ms. Frank: Loves artistic projects, is an easy marker
  • Mr. Chen: Very strict marker and rarely gives high grades
  • Ms. Patel: Focuses on creativity and originality, values effort
  • Mr. Thompson: Emphasizes structure and clarity, deducts for grammar errors
  • Ms. Rodriguez: Values evidence and citations, strict about sources
  • Mr. Kim: Likes detailed explanations, wants thorough analysis
  • Ms. Williams: Appreciates personal voice and unique perspectives
  • Mr. Davis: Strict about deadlines, values organization and presentation

You can add additional notes about your teacher's preferences in the "Additional Notes" field that appears after selection.

Creating a Custom Profile

If none of the preset profiles match your teacher, select "Other":

  • Enter your teacher's name (e.g., "Mr. Smith" or "Dr. Johnson")
  • Describe their marking style in detail (e.g., "Very strict marker, deducts for vague details, wants perfect structure")
  • The more specific you are, the better the feedback will be

Assignment Instructions

You can provide assignment instructions in two ways:

  • Text: Paste the assignment instructions directly into the textarea
  • Image: Upload a photo of the rubric or assignment sheet

Your Draft

Submit your work in one of these formats:

  • Text: Paste your written assignment
  • Image: Upload a photo of your artwork, project, or handwritten work

The AI uses advanced vision capabilities to understand visual content, not just extract text. This makes it perfect for analyzing artwork, diagrams, and visual projects.

Understanding Your Analysis

Grade Estimate

The grade estimate is based on your teacher's marking style and the assignment requirements. Click "Explain Results" to see a detailed breakdown of how the grade was calculated.

Feedback Categories

Feedback is organized by category to help you understand what to improve:

  • Structure: Organization, flow, and logical progression
  • Clarity: How clearly your ideas are expressed
  • Evidence: Use of examples, citations, and supporting details
  • Style: Writing style, voice, and tone
  • Requirements: How well you met the assignment criteria

Generate Rewrite

Output Format

Choose how you want the rewrite formatted:

  • Essay Format: Well-structured essay with introduction, body paragraphs, and conclusion
  • Paragraph Format: Flowing paragraphs with smooth transitions
  • Bullet Points: Structured list with clear, concise points
  • Other: Describe your preferred format (e.g., "numbered list", "outline format")

Rewrite Strength

Adjust the slider to control how much the AI changes your work:

  • Light (1-1.5): Gentle polish while maintaining your original voice
  • Balanced (1.5-2.5): Clear rewrite with improvements to structure and clarity
  • Max (2.5-3): Full makeover with significant improvements

ðŸ’¡ Tip: Start with "Balanced" and adjust based on how much change you want. You can always refine with feedback.

Refining with Feedback

If you're not satisfied with the analysis or rewrite, use the feedback section:

  • Be specific about what you want to change
  • Mention particular sections or aspects you'd like improved
  • You can refine multiple times until you're happy

Example feedback: "Make the introduction more engaging" or "Add more evidence to support the third point"

Best Practices

  • Be as detailed as possible when describing your teacher's marking style
  • Include the full rubric or assignment instructions for best results
  • Review the feedback carefully and use it to learn, not just to get a better grade
  • Remember to check your institution's academic integrity policies
  • Use OptimizeAI as a learning tool to understand what makes good writing
